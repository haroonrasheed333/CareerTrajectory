Want to work on advanced information-retrieval algorithms, massive scalability infrastructure and storage solutions, and large-scale applications for users on web and mobile devices? Apply now. LinkedIn is seeking world-class engineers to participate in building the world's most sophisticated, distributed, Java platform capable of serving billions of page views, scaling up to 238 million LinkedIn members and more, and supporting revenue lines of several hundred million dollars each. We’re headquartered in Mountain View, CA. Come join us and make an impact!

We are hiring in the following technology areas:
● Applications: Bring your experience building APIs and user-centric online applications, scaling performance and 24x7 reliability.
● Front-End Engineering: You’ll build the client web components (HTML, CSS, Javascript, JSP, YUI/JQuery) and server-side presentation tier in Java, JRuby, or Grails.
● Mobile Applications: Bring your rich experience building native and HTML5 applications for Android, iPhone, iPad, or Blackberry.
● Systems and Infrastructure: Building massive scalability infrastructure and storage solutions requires you to understand frameworks for caching, queuing and distributed data storage. Bring your deep understanding of multi-threading, concurrency, caching, and parallel processing technologies.
● Data Analysis, Data Mining and Machine Learning: To draw insights from our massive semi-structured and graph datasets, you need experience in data mining, information retrieval and/or machine learning and a strong systems orientation. Ideally, you have experience with MapReduce (e.g. Hadoop), and search engine internals (e.g. Lucene/SOLR).
● Tools Engineering: You’ll develop internal command line and web based tools to automate development, deployment, management and monitoring of our 24x7 online site.
● Performance Engineering: You’re an expert in finding bottlenecks. Bring your experience building performance benchmarks, analyzing and tuning HTTP interactions and storage systems like Oracle, Voldemort, BerkeleyDB etc.
